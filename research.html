Research

I am biologist surviving among statistical physicists and applied mathematicians. More accurately my research interests are at the intersection of biology, statistical physics and applied mathematics. Broadly I am interested in understanding behaviour and evolution of living systems using quantitative approaches. My doctoral advisor is Prof. Sitabhra Sinha

Projects

Classification of mosquito larvae using swim kinematics data

As a research assistant at TIGS, Bengaluru with Dr. Sonia Sen, I collected video recordings of larvae from three species and extracted motion trajectories. Implemented tracking and pre-processing pipelines for kinematic data. Applied classification algorithms to distinguish species based on swimming dynamics.

Master's Thesis

Effects of arena geometry on fish collective movement.
Advisor: Prof. Vishwesha Guttal, Center for Ecological Sceinces, IISc Bengaluru.
Co-advisor: Prof. Anuradha Bhat, Department of Biological Sciences, IISER Kolkata.
I looked at how arena geometry influences fish interactions and schooling be haviour. Performed trajectory tracking and quantitative analysis of fish movement to develop data-driven interaction models across different geometries. Analyzed both individual- and group-level behavioural metrics to characterize geometry-dependent collective dynamics Master's Thesis.
